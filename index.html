VividFace: High-Quality and Efficient One-Step Diffusion For Video Face Enhancement

Shulian Zhang1,2, Yong Guo2, Long Peng2, Ziyang Wang1, Ye Chen1, Jie Li1,2, Wenbo Li2, Xiao Zhang2, Yulun Zhang2, Jian Chen1
1South China University of China
2Huawei
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv
The left side shows a visual comparison between VividFace and existing video face restoration methods, illustrating that VividFace produces highly realistic and visually pleasing human eyes. The right side compares model inference time, parameter count, and IDS performance across different methods. VividFace achieves best performance, fastest speed, and comparable model parameter.

Abstract

Video Face Enhancement (VFE) aims to restore high-quality facial regions from degraded video sequences, enabling a wide range of practical applications. Despite substantial progress in the field, current methods that primarily rely on video super-resolution and generative frameworks continue to face three fundamental challenges: (1) computational inefficiency caused by iterative multi-step denoising in diffusion models; (2) faithfully modeling intricate facial textures while preserving temporal consistency; and (3) limited model generalization due to the lack of high-quality face video training data.

To address these challenges, we propose VividFace, a novel and efficient one-step diffusion framework for VFE. Built upon the pretrained WANX video generation model, VividFace reformulates the traditional multi-step diffusion process as a single-step flow matching paradigm that directly maps degraded inputs to high-quality outputs with significantly reduced inference time.

To enhance facial detail recovery, we introduce a Joint Latent-Pixel Face-Focused Training strategy that constructs spatiotemporally aligned facial masks to guide optimization toward critical facial regions in both latent and pixel spaces. Furthermore, we develop an MLLM-driven automated filtering pipeline that produces MLLM-Face90, a meticulously curated high-quality face video dataset, ensuring models learn from photorealistic facial textures. Extensive experiments demonstrate that VividFace achieves superior performance in perceptual quality, identity preservation, and temporal consistency across both synthetic and real-world benchmarks. We will publicly release our code, models, and dataset to support future research.

Pipeline

Overview of our VividFace framework. VividFace is a face-aware one-step video face enhancement framework built upon a flow-matching formulation. Given degraded input frames, the model first encodes them into the latent space, where a DiT-based network learns a single-step velocity field to transform degraded latent representations into high-quality ones. To guide the enhancement process, facial masks are extracted in the pixel space and then geometrically aligned to the latent space to match the VAE’s spatiotemporal compression, providing structure-aware supervision during latent optimization. In the second stage, the restored latent representations are decoded back to the pixel space, where further refinement is performed to enhance fine-grained facial details and perceptual quality. The same face-aware guidance is applied in the pixel space to ensure consistent structural enhancement. This two-stage design enables VividFace to achieve efficient and high-quality restoration while preserving temporal consistency and facial fidelity.
